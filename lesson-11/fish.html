An unsettled day for the majority of the U.S.

By Ze Old Cat Lady

The week moving towards Thanksgiving Day will continue with unsettled weather on Tuesday for much of the nation.

The wintry storms that can be seen in the Northwest and the Rockies will spread into the Plains as snow and ice turning to rain as it moves towards the Gulf Coast. From eastern Texas and Louisiana, the chances of severe thunderstorms and possible flooding will move north into the state of Missouri bringing a constant threat to the region throughout Tuesday.

The East Coast should remain dry and warm with a few clouds in the sky as the afternoon highs remain in the 50s and 60s from the Mid-Atlantic to the Southeast corner of the U.S.

An unsettled week will continue for those in the western and central portions of the U.S. on Tuesday with the West Coast seeing snow in the north turning to showers in California.

Sacramento will experience a day of showers and gusting winds with a high of 53 as snow moves through the higher elevations of the state. Snow will move through the Pacific Northwest where snow will be seen by many but Seattle should experience a few sunny spells along with a high of 43.

The majority of Montana and Idaho should remain dry as another band of snow and ice moves through the interior portions of the NOrthwest into the Northern and Central Plains.
